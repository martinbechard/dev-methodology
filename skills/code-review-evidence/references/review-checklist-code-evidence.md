# Code Review Evidence Checklist

## Completion Format

For every applicable question record the checklist identifier, status, cited evidence, factual observation, confidence, and missing evidence. Use supported, contradicted, unknown, or not applicable as the status.

## Scope And Skill Selection

- Question: Does the review identify the exact changed scope and intended behavior?
- Question: Does the review load every applicable generic, language, framework, tier, persistence, and test skill?
- Question: Does each detailed rule come from an applicable coding skill or project instruction instead of being invented by the reviewer?

## Evidence Quality

- Question: Does every observation cite the smallest useful file, symbol, location, command, or test result?
- Question: Are observed facts separated from inference and final judgment?
- Question: Are contradictions, unavailable evidence, skipped checks, and uncertainty preserved?
- Question: Does test evidence identify what behavior or boundary it actually verifies?

## Synthesis

- Question: Is every finding derived from contradicted or materially unknown evidence?
- Question: Does every finding state the impact, correction, and authority for the expected behavior?
- Question: Are severity and priority tied to real impact rather than style preference?
- Question: Does the review report material passes, open questions, and residual risk without overstating coverage?
